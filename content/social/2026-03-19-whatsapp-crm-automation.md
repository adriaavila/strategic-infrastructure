## Reddit post

**Title:** If your business gets leads on WhatsApp, manual follow-up is probably costing you sales

A lot of small businesses and service teams already get real buying-intent conversations through WhatsApp, but still manage them manually.

That usually creates the same problems:
- leads never make it into the CRM
- two people answer the same prospect
- quote requests get delayed
- follow-up depends on someone remembering
- there’s no clean view of the pipeline

What has worked better in practice is connecting WhatsApp to the CRM so every relevant conversation becomes a trackable opportunity instead of just another chat.

A solid setup usually does a few things well:
- creates the lead automatically
- captures key info early
- tags intent (support, quote, demo, purchase, etc.)
- assigns the right person
- triggers follow-ups if there’s no response
- keeps the full conversation history visible

The biggest win is not “automation” by itself. It’s response speed and consistency. A lot of deals don’t close on the first message, but they do close when follow-up happens at the right time.

For businesses getting steady inbound messages every week, this tends to be less about adding more staff and more about building better commercial infrastructure.
